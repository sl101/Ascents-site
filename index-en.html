Elbrus from the North - Eastern Top

Elbrus is the highest point in Europe, which by its nature is a long extinct stratovolcano. The mountain has two peaks (5642 and 5621 meters), separated by a saddle. We will climb to the lower, eastern peak. Big the area of the slopes is under the glaciers. Therefore, to climb Elbrus from the north, you must have a good physical training, basic skills of using an ice ax and crampons, movement in a bundle are welcomed.

If you do not have such experience yet, then in the first days of our program there will be an opportunity to learn the basics of technology high-altitude mountaineering. A couple of extra days have been added to the route for better acclimatization and in case bad weather. After all, these are two of the most important factors in climbing such difficulty.

Climbing on Elbrus from the South by classic

Elbrus from the south is the dream of many climbers and travelers. Not everyone can boast of climbing to the highest peak not only in Russia, but also in Europe. Our trip is divided into two parts: preparatory, which runs through the Elbrus region and allows the body to acclimatize to high altitudes, and, in fact, the very ascent to Elbrus.

This is an ascent route from the south to the western peak. If you are new to the big mountains, we recommend choosing exactly this route. During the program, you will learn the basics of mountaineering techniques needed to climb the classic path. The entire route is very rich and interesting, because the paths pass through the most picturesque and amazing places The Caucasus. Join us!

Climbing on Kazbek and Elbrus

Climbing Kazbek and Elbrus is a unique opportunity to climb two times in one trip five-thousanders of the Caucasus: Kazbek (5033 m) - the most famous Georgian peak and Elbrus (5642 m) - the highest point Europe and the Caucasus Mountains. In addition to climbing, you can enjoy many picturesque places of the Caucasus, get acquainted with Georgian and Balkar flavor and kitchen!

Despite the duration of the program and the height of the peaks, Kazbek and Elbrus are technically relatively simple and available tops. The ability to walk in crampons and in a bundle and previous climbing experience in large mountains is extremely desirable. Please note that we have other climbing programs to the top of Kazbek.
